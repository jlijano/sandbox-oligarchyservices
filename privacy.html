Services Approach Privacy

Privacy

Analytics should be useful without being invasive.

Analytics are currently disabled in this project. When enabled, the preferred approach is privacy-friendly aggregate measurement rather than individual visitor profiling.

What may be measured

A privacy-friendly analytics provider can report page views, referrers, device category, browser, operating system, and approximate location. Approximate location is typically inferred from network information by the analytics provider or hosting platform.

What should be avoided

The site should avoid cross-site tracking, fingerprinting, precise location tracking, unnecessary cookies, and collecting personal data that is not needed for site improvement.

Consent and disclosure

If analytics remains cookie-free and aggregate-only, a notice may be enough in some jurisdictions. If cookies, advertising features, user identifiers, or Google Analytics are added, a consent banner and legal review may be required depending on visitors' locations.

Local opt-out

Visitors can opt out of the optional analytics loader on this device. This setting is stored locally in the browser.
