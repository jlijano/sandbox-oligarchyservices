Privacy

Analytics and visitor measurement should stay practical and transparent.

Analytics are currently disabled. If enabled later, the preferred approach is aggregate, privacy-friendly measurement rather than individual visitor profiling.

What may be measured

A privacy-friendly analytics provider can report page views, referrers, device category, browser, operating system, and approximate location. Approximate location is typically inferred from network information by the provider or host.

What should be avoided

The site should avoid cross-site tracking, fingerprinting, precise location tracking, advertising identifiers, and unnecessary personal data collection.

Consent and disclosure

If analytics remains cookie-free and aggregate-only, a notice may be enough in some jurisdictions. If cookies, advertising features, user identifiers, or Google Analytics are added, a consent banner and legal review may be required depending on visitor locations.

Local opt-out

Visitors can opt out of the optional analytics loader on this device. This setting is stored locally in the browser.
